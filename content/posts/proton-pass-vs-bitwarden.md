## The obvious case for consolidating on Pass

*One vendor, one bill, one trust relationship, seamless integration with the rest of Proton. Give the argument fairly before you argue against it.*

## Why that same consolidation is the argument against

*Single point of failure — account compromise, account lockout, vendor direction changes. The case for deliberate separation.*

## What each does that the other doesn't

*Bitwarden Family's structure (six users, shared collections) vs Proton Pass's features (hide-my-email aliases, Proton tie-in). Concrete differences, not vibes.*

## How I actually split them

*Which credentials live where and why. Work vs personal, family-shared vs solo, low-sensitivity vs high. The reasoning behind each allocation.*

## Who should ignore this post

*If most people should just pick one and commit, say so. This post is for the subset for whom the decentralisation argument genuinely lands.*
